What is human evolution?

The extended period of change that separated humans from their apelike forebears is known as human evolution. According to scientific research, the physical characteristics and behavioural features that all humans share have their roots in apelike ancestors and have been evolving for around six million years.

Human Evolution Timeline

  • 55 million years ago

  • Evolution starts with the earliest primates.

  • 8 to 6 million years ago

  • The earliest gorillas emerge. Following this, the various chimpanzee and human lineages start to diverge from one another.

  • 5.8 million years ago

  • As the earliest known human ancestor is thought to have started walking on two legs, bipedalism is introduced. The species Orrorin tugenensis is descended from this ancestor.

  • 5.5 million years ago

  • Ardipithecus, a primate, has characteristics with chimps and gorillas. The habitat of this species is forests.

  • 4 million years ago

  • Australopithecines, a species of primate, appear. These primates walk on two back legs and have brains that are roughly the same size of chimpanzees. The first known human ancestors to inhabit the Savannah were the Australopithecines.

  • 3.2 million years ago

  • Australopithecus afarensis was a species that lived in Hadar, Ethiopia, as evidenced by a collection of fossilized skeletons found in East Africa in 1974. Since then, this specimen has gone by the name Lucy.

  • 2.7 million years ago

  • During this period, Paranthropus, a primate species, thrived in grasslands and woodlands. These primates possessed enormous jaws that they used to chew on rough plant material and roots. This species vanished about 1.2 million years ago.

  • 2.5 million years ago

  • Homo habilis, the species, first appears. The face of this species is notably less projecting yet nevertheless retains many characteristics with apes. During this time, hominids also started using stone tools more often, which they made by breaking stones. At this stage in the evolution of humans, certain hominids also started consuming a lot of meat. Due to the increase in meat eating and the resulting increase in energy, hominids were able to grow their brains bigger.

  • 2 million years ago

  • In Africa, remains of the Homo ergaster primate from this era have been discovered. Compared to earlier species, this primate had a larger brain.

  • 1.8 to 1.5 million years ago

  • The Homo erectus species is discovered in Asia. The earliest living examples of hunter-gatherers are these primates. They also happened to be the first in substantial numbers to travel outside of Africa. This species' brain is even larger, with a volume of about 1000 cm3.

  • 1.6 million years ago

  • Based on relics discovered in Kenya, it is thought that this period of time saw the earliest usage of fire. More sophisticated stone tools were also being developed and used at this period. The majority of early humans continued to use these tools up until about 100,000 years ago.

  • 600,000 years ago

  • The Homo Heidelbergensis species is currently found throughout Africa and Europe. The brain size of this primate species was quite similar to our own.

  • 500,000 years ago

  • This is the period when the earliest signs of intentionally constructed shelters first appeared. The homes were made of wood, and they were discovered in Japan close to Chichibu.

  • 400,000 years ago

  • The first humans began using spears for hunting.

  • 325,000 years ago

  • Human footprints first appeared 325,000 years ago, according to archaeological findings. These prints are thought to be those of three individuals who descended an Italian volcano's slopes.

  • 280,000 years ago

  • The first sophisticated grinding and cutting stones are made.

  • 230,000 years ago

  • Neanderthals start to appear around this time. They can be found all over Europe, from Iran to Britain. When the arrival of modern humans about 280,000 years ago, they finally go extinct.

  • 195,000 years ago

  • Homo sapiens, or modern humans initially appear at this time. Human migration spans Asia and Europe. This is the time frame for the earliest indications of Homo sapiens remains. Two human skulls were among the bones that were discovered in Ethiopia. The average human brain's volume at this period was 1350 cm3.

  • 170,000 years ago

  • It is thought that around this period, Africa was home to the earliest direct ancestor for all the modern humans. This ancestor is known as Mitochondrial Eve by scientists.

  • 150,000 years ago

  • Humans are able to speak for the first time. Evidence suggests that at this time, Homo sapiens began to create a sophisticated system of symbolism and speech.

  • 140,000 years ago

  • Long-distance trading was first observed 140,000 years ago.

  • 50,000 years ago

  • Human culture begins to evolve at a significantly faster rate at this stage in the human evolution timeline. A few cultural shifts included the adoption of ritualistic burial practices, the production of clothing made out of animal skin, and the development of more clever, efficient hunting methods.

  • 33,000 years ago

  • The earliest indications of cave art date around 33,000 years ago. At this time, Homo erectus goes extinct entirely in Asia and becomes replaced by the humans we know today.

  • 18,000 years ago

  • On Flores, an Indonesian island, there is a species of diminutive, prehistoric humans known as Homo Floresiensis. These prehistoric people are little over a meter tall, and their brains are around the same size as chimpanzees'. But unlike chimps, these people did have sophisticated stone tools.

  • 12,000 years ago

  • The Americas become home to modern people.

  • 10,000 years ago

  • Agriculture starts to grow and swiftly spreads over the world. The development of the first ever villages is related to the expansion of agriculture. It's also believed that at this time, dogs were initially domesticated and people kept them as pets.

  • 5,500 years ago

  • The Bronze Age starts and the Stone Age ends. As a new era begins, humanity start using copper and tin tools as opposed to the stone ones they had previously used.

  • 5,000 years ago

  • The first known instance of writing comes from this era.

  • 4,000 to 3,500 years ago

  • The Mesopotamian Ancient Sumerians create the world's first civilization.
